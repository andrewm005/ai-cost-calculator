About MiniMax-01

MiniMax-01 is a mid-tier model in the MiniMax lineup, priced at $0.20 per 1M input tokens and $1.10 per 1M output tokens. It is the kind of model most teams end up defaulting to once they have measured their workloads: cheap enough to run at moderate volume, capable enough that the answer quality does not require a flagship. MiniMax generally publishes a budget option below this model and a flagship above it, and MiniMax-01 is the middle step in that ladder.

At $0.20 per 1M input tokens and $1.10 per 1M output tokens, the cost of a single call is dominated by output unless the workload is very output-heavy or very input-heavy in unusual ways. Output is priced noticeably above input (5.5x), reflecting the typical chat-shape where the model spends more compute on generation than on reading the prompt. The context window is about 1M tokens, large enough to hold a substantial codebase, a long document, or an extended multi-turn conversation without truncation. What makes MiniMax-01 different

MiniMax (Shanghai-based MiniMax, founded 2021) builds the MiniMax lineup with three priorities: very long context windows, Chinese-English bilingual fluency, and competitive per-token pricing against US frontier models. The stack ships behind the same OpenRouter endpoint used for GPT-4o and Claude Sonnet 4, so a request that hits MiniMax-01 looks like any other model call from the application side. The trade-off is brand recognition: MiniMax does not have the marketing footprint of OpenAI or Anthropic, so the same quality per dollar gets less press attention, but the per-token prices are routinely 3-10x below US flagship tiers.

MiniMax-01 is built for a 1M-token context window that holds an entire codebase or a long multi-turn conversation without truncation. It fits well in long-context chat where the conversation history is the asset (customer-support transcripts, multi-hour tutoring sessions, agent traces that need to be replayed verbatim), in Chinese-English bilingual work where the model has been post-trained on both languages at production scale, and in any workload where the per-token cost dominates the bill and the team can route around MiniMax's lower brand recognition. The MiniMax m2 family is the production workhorse; MiniMax-M1 and MiniMax-01 are the long-context picks when the workload genuinely needs a million tokens in the prompt.

Frequently asked questions

How much does MiniMax-01 cost per 1M tokens?

MiniMax-01 costs $0.20 per 1M input tokens and $1.10 per 1M output tokens on OpenRouter. A typical chat run (1,000 input, 500 output) costs about $0.0008; a long-context job (50,000 input, 5,000 output) costs about $0.015.

What is the context window for MiniMax-01?

The context window for MiniMax-01 is 1,000,192 tokens, large enough to hold a substantial codebase or a long document without chunking. Anything longer has to be chunked or summarized before it fits.

Does MiniMax-01 support tool use / function calling?
