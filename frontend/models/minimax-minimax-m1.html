About MiniMax M1

MiniMax M1 is a mid-tier model in the MiniMax lineup, priced at $0.40 per 1M input tokens and $2.20 per 1M output tokens. It is the kind of model most teams end up defaulting to once they have measured their workloads: cheap enough to run at moderate volume, capable enough that the answer quality does not require a flagship. MiniMax generally publishes a budget option below this model and a flagship above it, and MiniMax M1 is the middle step in that ladder.

At $0.40 per 1M input tokens and $2.20 per 1M output tokens, the cost of a single call is dominated by output unless the workload is very output-heavy or very input-heavy in unusual ways. Output is priced noticeably above input (5.5x), reflecting the typical chat-shape where the model spends more compute on generation than on reading the prompt. The context window is about 1M tokens, large enough to hold a substantial codebase, a long document, or an extended multi-turn conversation without truncation. What makes MiniMax M1 different

MiniMax (Shanghai-based MiniMax, founded 2021) builds the MiniMax lineup with three priorities: very long context windows, Chinese-English bilingual fluency, and competitive per-token pricing against US frontier models. The stack ships behind the same OpenRouter endpoint used for GPT-4o and Claude Sonnet 4, so a request that hits MiniMax M1 looks like any other model call from the application side. The trade-off is brand recognition: MiniMax does not have the marketing footprint of OpenAI or Anthropic, so the same quality per dollar gets less press attention, but the per-token prices are routinely 3-10x below US flagship tiers.

MiniMax M1 is built for a 1M-token context window that holds an entire codebase or a long multi-turn conversation without truncation. It fits well in long-context chat where the conversation history is the asset (customer-support transcripts, multi-hour tutoring sessions, agent traces that need to be replayed verbatim), in Chinese-English bilingual work where the model has been post-trained on both languages at production scale, and in any workload where the per-token cost dominates the bill and the team can route around MiniMax's lower brand recognition. The MiniMax m2 family is the production workhorse; MiniMax-M1 and MiniMax-01 are the long-context picks when the workload genuinely needs a million tokens in the prompt.

For a US-headquartered team running purely US-hosted workloads the procurement argument can outweigh the price advantage; for a cost-sensitive workload where the answer quality is acceptable, MiniMax M1 is one of the stronger picks at this tier.

Frequently asked questions

How much does MiniMax M1 cost per 1M tokens?

MiniMax M1 costs $0.40 per 1M input tokens and $2.20 per 1M output tokens on OpenRouter. A typical chat run (1,000 input, 500 output) costs about $0.0015; a long-context job (50,000 input, 5,000 output) costs about $0.031.

What is the context window for MiniMax M1?

The context window for MiniMax M1 is 1,000,000 tokens, large enough to hold a substantial codebase or a long document without chunking. Anything longer has to be chunked or summarized before it fits.

Does MiniMax M1 support tool use / function calling?

OpenRouter does not publish a tool-use flag for MiniMax M1 on this listing, so assume no and validate in your own integration. If the upstream feed adds the flag, the next six-hourly refresh will surface it here.

Is MiniMax M1 cheaper than Llama 3.1 70B Instruct?

MiniMax M1 and Llama 3.1 70B Instruct are priced identically on OpenRouter ($0.40 per 1M input, $2.20 per 1M output); the choice between them comes down to capability, not cost. See the Llama 3.1 70B Instruct model page for the full price table and workload examples.

How much does it cost to run MiniMax M1 for chatbot?

A typical chatbot run (1,000 input + 500 output) on MiniMax M1 costs about $0.0015 per call. At 10,000 runs/month that is roughly $15.00. The bulk of that is output at $2.20 per 1M tokens.
