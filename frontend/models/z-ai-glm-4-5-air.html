About GLM 4.5 Air

GLM 4.5 Air is a budget-tier model on OpenRouter, priced at $0.13 per 1M input tokens and $0.85 per 1M output tokens. It is the right pick for high-volume work where the cost per call matters more than the absolute best answer quality: bulk classification, extraction pipelines, simple chat agents, and the kind of background work that fires thousands of times a day. It is also a sensible default for prototypes where the team has not yet decided which model they want to standardize on, since the bill will be small even if the volume is high.

At $0.13 per 1M input tokens and $0.85 per 1M output tokens, the cost of a single call is dominated by output unless the workload is very output-heavy or very input-heavy in unusual ways. Output is priced noticeably above input (6.5x), reflecting the typical chat-shape where the model spends more compute on generation than on reading the prompt. The context window is about 131K tokens, which covers most single-turn chat and short retrieval tasks but requires chunking for long documents. What makes GLM 4.5 Air different

Z.AI (Zhipu AI, founded 2019 in Beijing) ships the GLM line as their primary frontier family. GLM stands for General Language Model, and the family has been the company's flagship open-weights push since the GLM-130B release in 2022. The current GLM 4.5 Air sits inside that lineage: same OpenRouter endpoint as any other model, but tuned and post-trained against Zhipu's own data, with multimodal variants (the GLM 4.5 Air family often includes a -v version) handling images and the text-only versions hitting lower per-token prices. For US-headquartered teams, GLM is the less-marketed sibling of Qwen and DeepSeek in the Chinese open-weights space; for China-headquartered teams and for multilingual workloads, it is one of the default picks.

GLM 4.5 Air is built for a budget-tier price tuned for high-volume classification, extraction, and routing layers where the per-call cost dominates the bill. It fits well in Chinese language workloads where Zhipu's training data gives it an edge over US-trained models on idiomatic Chinese, in document-understanding pipelines that need multimodal input without paying GPT-4o prices, and in any workload that wants open-weights lineage (Zhipu has historically published GLM weights under a permissive license) without giving up production-grade API availability on OpenRouter.
