About MiniMax M2.7

MiniMax M2.7 is a mid-tier model in the MiniMax lineup, priced at $0.24 per 1M input tokens and $0.96 per 1M output tokens. It is the kind of model most teams end up defaulting to once they have measured their workloads: cheap enough to run at moderate volume, capable enough that the answer quality does not require a flagship. MiniMax generally publishes a budget option below this model and a flagship above it, and MiniMax M2.7 is the middle step in that ladder.

At $0.24 per 1M input tokens and $0.96 per 1M output tokens, the cost of a single call is dominated by output unless the workload is very output-heavy or very input-heavy in unusual ways. Output is priced noticeably above input (4.0x), reflecting the typical chat-shape where the model spends more compute on generation than on reading the prompt. The context window is about 204K tokens, suitable for long documents and extended agent loops without having to chunk the input. What makes MiniMax M2.7 different

MiniMax (Shanghai-based MiniMax, founded 2021) builds the MiniMax lineup with three priorities: very long context windows, Chinese-English bilingual fluency, and competitive per-token pricing against US frontier models. The stack ships behind the same OpenRouter endpoint used for GPT-4o and Claude Sonnet 4, so a request that hits MiniMax M2.7 looks like any other model call from the application side. The trade-off is brand recognition: MiniMax does not have the marketing footprint of OpenAI or Anthropic, so the same quality per dollar gets less press attention, but the per-token prices are routinely 3-10x below US flagship tiers.

MiniMax M2.7 is built for a 200K-token context window sized for long documents and extended agent loops without chunking. It fits well in long-context chat where the conversation history is the asset (customer-support transcripts, multi-hour tutoring sessions, agent traces that need to be replayed verbatim), in Chinese-English bilingual work where the model has been post-trained on both languages at production scale, and in any workload where the per-token cost dominates the bill and the team can route around MiniMax's lower brand recognition. The MiniMax m2 family is the production workhorse; MiniMax-M1 and MiniMax-01 are the long-context picks when the workload genuinely needs a million tokens in the prompt.

Frequently asked questions

What is the context window for MiniMax M2.7?
