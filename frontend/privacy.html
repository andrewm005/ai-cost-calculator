The short version

AI Cost Calculator is a free AI token-cost calculator. It runs almost entirely in your browser. We don't have accounts, we don't set tracking cookies, we don't run third-party analytics, and we don't store the prompts or numbers you put into the calculator. The only thing we ask your browser to remember is an optional list of models you've starred as favorites. You can clear them any time.

The rest of this page spells out exactly what that means in legal terms, what counts as a "third party," and what rights you have.

What we collect

Almost nothing. Specifically:

  • Optional: a list of model IDs you star as "favorites." This is stored only in your browser's localStorage under the key aicostcalculator.favorites.v1. It is not transmitted to our servers. Clear it any time from your browser settings or by clicking the ☆ button until all favorites are removed.
  • Server access logs. When your browser fetches a price list or calculates a cost, the request goes through Cloudflare's edge network and reaches our backend. Cloudflare and our backend each record standard HTTP request metadata: timestamp, path, status code, and IP address. These logs are used only to debug errors and to apply rate limits. We do not sell, share, or analyze them.

What we do NOT collect

To be explicit about what this site does not do:

  • No Google Analytics, Plausible, Fathom, or any other pageview tracker.
  • No Facebook Pixel, LinkedIn Insight, or any ad-network pixel.
  • No Sentry, Datadog, or any error-reporting service that ships your browser state to a third party.
  • No session-replay tools (FullStory, Hotjar, etc.).
  • No cookies set by this site. Period.
  • No fingerprinting, no canvas tracking, no font enumeration, no device-class heuristics, no cross-site identifiers.
  • No email collection. No newsletter opt-in. No marketing database. No "we'll send you updates."

How we use what little we have

The favorites list stays in your browser. We use it to restore your starred models when you reload the page; that's it.

The server access logs are used to:

  • Diagnose errors (e.g. "why is the OpenRouter refresh failing for this one user").
  • Detect abuse, including automated scrapers hammering the API or someone trying to break in. When that happens we may block the IP at the network layer.
  • Cap request rates so a single user can't accidentally DoS the service for everyone else.

We do not use logs to build user profiles, to advertise to you, to train AI models, or to share with third parties. We do not correlate your request with any other data source.

Cookies, localStorage, and tracking

Cookies: as of June 2026, this site does not set any cookies itself. We may add Google AdSense for ad-supported operation in the future; if we do, AdSense will set its own cookies for ad personalization. We will update this section before activating any advertising.

localStorage: the only thing we use localStorage for is the optional favorites list described above. localStorage is per-origin, never sent over the network, and you can wipe it from your browser's developer tools (Application → Storage → Clear site data) any time.

Tracking pixels, beacons, third-party scripts: none today. Same AdSense caveat as above: if we add advertising, ads load with their own tracking. We will not add any other third-party trackers, retargeting pixels, or session-replay tools.

Third-party services

For full transparency, here are every external service this site touches:

  • Google Fonts (fonts.google.com). It serves the DM Serif serves the DM Serif Display font CSS and font files. Google may log your IP address when these are requested. You can avoid this by using a browser extension that blocks third-party requests, or by reading the page with JavaScript disabled (the page will fall back to system fonts).
  • OpenRouter (openrouter.ai). Our backend pulls their public model catalog every 6 hours to keep our pricing data current. We do not send any user data (prompts, results, identifiers) to OpenRouter.
  • Cloudflare (cloudflare.com). It hosts the static site on its edge network and runs the backend on Cloudflare Workers. Cloudflare sees connection metadata (your IP, the path you requested, the response status, TLS handshake details) but not the contents of your calculations. Cloudflare's own privacy policy applies to what they log: cloudflare.com/privacypolicy.

When you click a link to a third-party site (e.g. to a vendor's pricing page or to OpenRouter), that site's privacy policy applies, not this one.

Data retention

localStorage favorites: live in your browser until you clear them. We do not retain a copy on any server.

Server access logs: retained for 30 days, then rolled off. Used only for debugging and abuse detection during that window.

Calculations: computed on the fly, returned to your browser, and forgotten. We do not log the inputs or outputs of any calculation.

Your rights (GDPR, CCPA, and friends)

If you're in the EU, UK, California, or anywhere else with a comprehensive data-protection law, your rights under those laws are mostly satisfied by default because we hold almost no data about you. Specifically:

  • Right to access: we have nothing to show you beyond the server access logs of your own requests, and we don't have a way to look those up by identifier without disproportionate effort. If you need a copy for a legal matter, contact us and we'll do our best.
  • Right to deletion: the only thing you might want deleted is your server access log entries, which already auto-delete after 30 days. Your localStorage favorites are entirely under your control. Clear them from your browser any time.
  • Right to portability: your favorites list is already in your browser in a plain JSON format. You can copy it out of devtools (Application → Local Storage → aicostcalculator.favorites.v1) any time.
  • Right to object: we don't do automated decision-making or profiling, so there's nothing to object to.
  • Right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority: if you believe we're mishandling your data, contact us first so we can fix it. You're also free to lodge a complaint with your local data-protection authority.

California-specific (CCPA/CPRA): we do not sell or share personal information. We do not collect categories of personal information as defined by the CCPA.

EU-specific (GDPR): the data controller is the individual operator of AI Cost Calculator. Contact details below. The legal basis for processing the server access logs is our legitimate interest in keeping the service secure and operational (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR).

Children's privacy

AI Cost Calculator is not directed at children under 13 (or under 16 in the EU). We do not knowingly collect information from children. If you believe a child has used this service and we hold data about them, contact us and we'll delete it.
